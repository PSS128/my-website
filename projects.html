Differential Demographics Effect (DDE)

AIBRT · Behavioral Research

The paper introduces the Differential Demographics Effect (DDE), a form of online influence in which the same content can produce predictable differences across demographic groups when response patterns and population proportions are known.

The analysis highlights why this kind of influence can be difficult to detect: because every group receives identical content, the resulting demographic margin can remain invisible to users and hard for authorities to trace.

Classifying Tennis Player Styles

Python · k-means · DBSCAN · Render

An interactive web app that scrapes pro tennis match data from Tennis Abstract and classifies each player's style across three dimensions: rally length, shot frequency, and winner types. Players are benchmarked against tour averages in real time.

Rally length — players with shorter average points are labeled First-Strike (aggressive, looks to finish early); longer points earn the Grinder label (patient, comfortable in extended rallies).

Shot frequency — players are labeled Likes to hit Forehand, Likes to hit Backhand, or Likes to hit Groundstrokes based on their forehand/backhand split relative to the tour average.

Shot variety & net play — players above the tour average in slices, dropshots, or lobs earn the Variety tag; those who approach the net more than average are labeled Likes to go to net.

Data sourced from the Tennis Abstract match charting project.

Pokémon Type Prediction & Analysis

Python · scikit-learn · Tableau · Kaggle

Two complementary analyses combined into one project. The prediction model trained Random Forest, Decision Tree, LDA, and KNN classifiers on 800+ Pokémon entries with 20+ attributes, achieving a ROC AUC of 0.69 and 28.2% accuracy — 5× above the random baseline.

The type analysis introduces two scoring metrics visualized in an interactive Tableau dashboard:

Cumulative Defense score — sum of all damage multipliers received by a type combination (1 = neutral, 2 = super-effective, 4 = double super-effective, 0.5 = resist, 0.25 = double resist, 0 = immune). A lower score is better, indicating more resistances and immunities.

Cumulative Coverage score — sum of the highest damage a type combination can deal against each individual type. A higher score is better, meaning the combination hits more types for super-effective damage.
